Make a call

How to Make a Call

  1. Be sure to call local offices instead of just the DC office. There are staffers in local offices whose responsibility it is to respond to constituents.
  2. Leave a message if you can’t speak to somebody. If all inboxes are full/phone lines down, try another office. If you can't leave your message at all by phone, send an email, then call back another day.
  3. Set aside time to make calls daily/weekly. Persistence is highly effective.

What to Say

  1. Make sure to leave your name and full address where you're registered to vote. Calls may be disregarded if you can’t prove your constituency.
  2. Name the issue you'd like to talk to them about.
  3. Name the action you want the representative to take on the issue.
  4. Briefly explain why you are calling about the issue. We've provided effective examples below if you're unsure of what to say. If you have personal/professional experience with the issue, make sure to mention it.
  5. You can also call to ask where your rep stands on an issue, or simply call to thank them for their support. You can also call to ask where your rep stands on an issue, or simply call to thank them for their support. where.

Support the Voting Rights Amendment Act

My name is ______, and I'm a constituent from City, State. Leave your full address and any other information the office asks for.

I’m calling to urge Sen/Rep _____ to support the Voting Rights Advancement Act, legislation that will protect our vulnerable communities from suppressive voting legislation and ensure that everyone in America has a voice in our democracy.

These laws are intentionally discriminatory on the basis of race, age, and disability and unconstitutionally disenfranchise millions of americans in these vulnerable communities. For Jeff Sessions and the Justice Department to dismiss the claim that suppressive voter laws are not crafted with the intention of discrimination is outrageous. A change in administration in no way invalidates thousands of pages of overwhelming evidence that proves this legislation deliberately suppresses minority voters.

It is unacceptable to continue to disenfranchise voters in our democracy, and I urge Sen/Rep _____ to support the Voting Rights Advancement act and to ensure that all voices can be heard. Thank you for your time.

My name is ______, and I'm a constituent from City, State. Leave your full address and any other information the office asks for.

I’m calling to urge Sen/Rep ____ to do everything in his/her power to oppose the lifting of Title IX protections for transgender students.

The reality is that there hasn’t been a single case of any student pretending to be transgender to gain access to a bathroom, and the real crime is turning our backs on vulnerable students who deserve to be protected under Title IX. This discriminatory decision leaves our most vulnerable children and teenagers at an even higher risk of bullying and stigmatization, and it is heinous that the Departments of Justice and Education are turning their backs and allowing this to happen in our schools.
