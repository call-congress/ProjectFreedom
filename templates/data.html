Instructions

How to Make a Call

  1. Be sure to call local offices instead of just the DC office. There are staffers in local offices who specifically respond to constituents.
  2. Leave a message if you can’t speak to somebody. If the inbox is full or phone line is down, try another office. If you can’t leave your message at all by phone, send an email, then call back another day.
  3. Set aside time to make calls daily/weekly. Persistence is highly effective.
  4. Briefly explain why you are calling about the issue. We’ve provided effective examples below if you’re unsure of what to say. If you have personal/professional experience with the issue, make sure to mention it. You can also call to ask where your rep stands on an issue, or simply call to thank them for their support.

What to Say

  1. Make sure to leave your name and full address where you’re registered to vote. Calls may be disregarded if you can’t prove your constituency.
  2. Name the issue you’d like to talk about.
  3. Name the action you want the representative to take on the issue.
  4. Briefly explain why you are calling. We’ve provided effective examples below if you’re unsure of what to say. If you have personal/professional experience with the issue, make sure to mention it.
  5. You can also call to ask where your rep stands on an issue, or simply call to thank them for their support.
